Target Reach

Defining the theoretical limits and the practical "Goldilocks Zone" for optimal robotic arm performance.

Theoretical Maximum: 400mm

The Limit

A sphere with radius 400mm centered at the first joint.

The Singularity Trap

At exactly 400mm, the arm is a straight line. It cannot extend further, and orientation adjustment is severely limited.

Motor Stress

Highest leverage point. A 0.5kg load here puts maximum torque on NEMA 23 base & NEMA 17 shoulder.

Realistic Working Reach: 200mm – 320mm

The "Goldilocks Zone" for accuracy, strength, and fluidity.

0 – 100mm Dead Zone

Self-Collision Risk

The arm cannot reach here without "biceps" or "forearm" colliding with the base.

200 – 300mm Sweet Spot

Best Accuracy & Advantage

Joints are at ~90°, providing optimal mechanical advantage and minimal bounce/vibration.

320mm Max Utility

Reliable Limit

Furthest point where wrist orientation (roll/pitch) is reliable without hitting a singularity.

350 – 400mm Danger Zone

Stall Risk

Best used only for momentary reaches (e.g., hitting a button). High risk of motor stalling.

Why Stay Within Realistic Reach?

A. Singularity Avoidance

At full extension (400mm), Inverse Kinematics math often fails, causing erratic jumps. Staying within 80% ensures "room to move."

B. Precision & "Jitter"

Microscopic play magnifies at distance. A 0.5mm shoulder gap becomes a 5mm wobble at 400mm. Working closer significantly reduces error.

C. Payload Capacity

Torque required at 400mm is DOUBLE that at 200mm. The "Sweet Spot" allows the NEMA 17 elbow to lift heavier objects without skipping steps.
