Primary Target: Specific Stiffness

The AI optimizes for the Stiffness-to-Mass Ratio (Specific Stiffness).

Math

Objective Function

Minimize Compliance — the mathematical inverse of maximizing stiffness.

Process

The Goal

Remove material from low-stress areas (beam centers) and reinforce high-stress areas (motor mount corners).

Constraint

Mass Target

Set to 30%–40% of the original bounding box — forcing the AI to find the most efficient "skeleton."

Segment-Specific Safety Factors

Different arm segments require different safety margins based on their load profile and failure risk.

Segment Recommended FOS Rationale
Base & Shoulder 2.5 – 3.0 High fatigue risk from NEMA 23 torque & constant vibration.
Forearm (Axis 4) 2.0 Balance of strength and speed; must stay light.
Wrist (Axis 5 & 6) 1.5 – 2.0 Minimal catastrophic failure risk; every gram saved helps the shoulder.

Modal Frequency Targets

A "hidden" optimization critical for 6-axis arms — long, thin arms tend to oscillate or shake when they stop moving.

Constraint

First Natural Frequency

> 30 Hz

The fundamental frequency must exceed this threshold to prevent low-frequency vibration.

AI Response

Structural Reinforcement

If the arm is too "bendy," the AI adds ribs or thickening to the structure — making the arm "snappier" and more stable, like stiffening a diving board.
