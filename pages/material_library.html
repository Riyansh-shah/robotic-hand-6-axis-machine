Defining the physical properties and economic constraints for generative optimization.

High-Performance Plastics (Desktop 3D Printing)

Industrial Standard (SLS)

Nylon 12 / PA11

Properties
  • Density ~1.01 g/cm³
  • Yield 48-52 MPa
  • Modulus 1.8-2.0 GPa
Economics & Usage
  • Raw Cost $50-$70 / kg
  • Service $0.35-$0.80 / g
  • Best for: Professional-grade joints.
Hobbyist Choice (FDM)

PETG / ABS

Properties
  • Density ~1.27 g/cm³
  • Yield 30-50 MPa
  • Modulus 2.0-2.1 GPa
Economics & Usage
  • Raw Cost $16-$28 / kg
  • Service N/A (Desktop)
  • Best for: Low-cost prototyping.
Avoid PLA (Creep Risk)
High Stiffness

PA-CF (Carbon Fiber)

Properties
  • Density ~1.08 g/cm³
  • Yield 70-110 MPa
  • Modulus 4.0-6.0 GPa
Economics & Usage
  • Raw Cost $55-$90 / kg
  • Service $1.00+ / g
  • Best for: Stiffest/Lightest forearm parts.

Metals (CNC or DMLS)

The Gold Standard (CNC)

Aluminum 6061-T6

Properties
  • Density 2.70 g/cm³
  • Yield 240-275 MPa
  • Modulus 68.9 GPa
Economics
  • Raw Cost $4.50-$5.50 / kg
  • Part Cost $50-$200 / part
  • Best for: Base & Shoulder (High Stress).
Additive Alloy (DMLS)

Aluminum AlSi10Mg

Properties
  • Density 2.68 g/cm³
  • Yield 220-240 MPa
  • Modulus ~70 GPa
Economics
  • Raw Cost $25-$45 / kg (Powder)
  • Part Cost $150-$500 / part
  • Best for: Complex generative shapes.
