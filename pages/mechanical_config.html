6-DOF Joint Layout

The articulated joint arrangement split into two functional sub-assemblies: Positioning and Orientation.

Positioning Sub-Assembly (Axes 1–3)

Responsible for placing the wrist at any point in 3D space.

1

Base / Waist

Yaw Rotation

Horizontal swing. Supports the entire weight of the robot above it.

2

Shoulder

Pitch Motion

The primary "lifter," directly counteracting gravity on the arm.

3

Elbow

Pitch Motion

Extends or retracts the arm to define the radial reach distance.

Orientation Sub-Assembly (Axes 4–6)

The "Spherical Wrist" — defines the tool pose without changing 3D position.

4

Wrist Roll

Forearm Rotation

Rotates the forearm. Essential for aligning welding torches or drills.

5

Wrist Pitch

Bend / Tilt

Tilts the end-effector up and down for approach angle control.

6

Tool Flange

Tool Roll

Final rotation that spins the gripper or tool directly.
