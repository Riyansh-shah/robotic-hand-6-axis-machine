Drivetrain System

The transmission ecosystem: stepper motors, gear reductions, and the critical role of backlash control.

Axis Drive Recommendations

Component Joint Role Drive Type Rationale
Axis 1 & 2 Base / Shoulder Belt Drive or Planetary Handles high torque; keeps the motor's weight centered.
Axis 3 Elbow Planetary or Cycloidal Needs high reduction in a small space to fight leverage.
Axis 4, 5, 6 Wrist Direct Drive (Servos) Minimum weight; speed is more important than raw torque.

Drive Types In Detail

Belt Drive

The "Flexible" Option

Uses timing belts (GT2 or HTD) and pulleys.

Pros

Easy to 3D print; allows offsetting the motor to reduce arm inertia.

Cons

Belts can stretch → backlash (slop) in movements.

Model belt path as Red Obstacle Geometry

Planetary Gearbox

The "Power" Option

Central "sun" gear spins "planet" gears inside a "ring" gear.

Pros

High torque density; ~98% efficient; compact form factor.

Cons

Hard to 3D print precisely; requires grease; backlash risk.

Use Fusion's "Spur Gear" script to generate

Cycloidal Drive

The "Pro" Option

Eccentric bearing wobbles a disc inside a ring of pins.

Pros

Zero backlash; up to 100:1 ratio; incredible shock resistance.

Cons

Complex design; requires very high 3D printing tolerances.

Pairs beautifully with generative aesthetic

Backlash: The "Silent Killer"

In a 6-axis arm, backlash is the tiny "wiggle" a joint has when the motor isn't moving.

The Problem

A tiny gap in your drivetrain gears at the shoulder amplifies dramatically at the tip:

Gap at Shoulder → 0.1mm → 2.0–5.0mm error at the tip of a 400mm arm
The Solution

If using 3D printed gears, use specialized gear patterns to minimize play:

Herringbone or Helical Gear Pattern

Generative Design Integration

How to properly model your drivetrain in Fusion 360's Generative Design workspace.

Preserve Shafts

Model the output shaft of your gearbox as a Preserve Geometry so the AI keeps this critical interface.

Obstacle Cylinders

Create "Keep-Out" cylinders around gears or pulleys so the AI doesn't build bone into spinning teeth.

Bearing Seats

Always preserve the ring where ball bearings sit. A generative structure is useless without press-fit bearings.
